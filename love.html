This website says a lot of things I don't say verbally.


I think I've already said that once before though.




I don't reckon I've ever told anyone in my life that I love them. At least not as long as I can remember - and certainly not in a wholesome, true-to-the-sense-of-the-word way.

I don't know why that is. At this point, it would almost be like breaking a perfect streak.

I don't think it's a bad thing. I know I feel... something that's probably love, but there's really just no value in saying something like that. But the whole point of love is that it's relational.


You can't really love by yourself. There's got to be a second party involved in some way.



And therein lies the problem.

How else could they know unless you tell them?

Actions can only go so far. So you try your best. You still have that perpetual look on your face, but you just carry on.


Even though I like that look on my face.

It's my brand. It's comfortable. It's more interesting than the less genuine alternative.


This may or may not actually be an issue. I really have no way to tell.

I'm quite self-aware of this reality. But that doesn't really change the matter.

And there's probably not a finite, objective answer to this. I'm sure Ann Landers would have an opinion.



Despite my general nonchalance on the topic (and, well, everything), this situation weighs upon me more than most things.

And I think I have to live with it. It feels out of my control, like talking to strangers in public. One's innate nature holds them back from such a simple task.

But it's there. It's always there. With or without voice.
